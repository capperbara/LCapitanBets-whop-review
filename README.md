## Understanding Sports Betting Categories

  

Since LCapitanBets covers various betting types, let me break down what you'll encounter:

  

### Primary Betting Categories

  

**Moneyline bets** require your chosen team to win the game outright. No spreads or handicaps involved. Favorites pay lower odds while underdogs offer better payouts.

  

**Point spread betting** is the most common type in American sports betting. The spread handicaps one team and favors another. For example, if the Lakers are -5.5 against the Warriors, the Lakers must win by 6 or more points for your bet to cash.

  

**Over/under betting** focuses on the total combined score of both teams. If an NBA game has a total of 220.5, you're betting whether both teams will score more or less than that combined.

  

### Advanced Betting Options

  

**Parlay bets** combine multiple selections into one ticket. All picks must win for the parlay to cash, but payouts are significantly higher. In the USA, parlays account for over 70% of each state's total sportsbook revenue monthly.

  

**Teaser bets** are modified parlays where you get more favorable point spreads in exchange for lower payouts. For example, you might move a 3-point spread to 9 points in your favor.

  

**Proposition bets** focus on specific statistical outcomes not related to the final score. Think "Will this player score over 25 points" or "Which team scores first."

  

**Futures betting** involves long-term wagers like "Who will win the Super Bowl" placed months before the event concludes.

  

**Live betting** allows you to place wagers while games are in progress, with odds constantly changing based on game flow.

  

### Sports Betting Legality Context

  

Before diving deeper into LCapitanBets, it's important to understand the current legal landscape.

  

The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) previously made sports betting illegal in most US states until 2018.

  

The Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association, opening the door for state-by-state legalization.

  

As of January 2024, 37 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico have operational legal sports betting.

  

30 states have legalized online sports betting, while 8 states restrict betting to physical sportsbook locations only.

  

Since PASPA was overturned, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports in the United States through May 2020 alone.
